## Trademark plan

### Why file at all

Registration is not required for trademark rights — use in commerce creates common-law rights. But federal registration gives you:
- Nationwide priority (common-law rights are geographic)
- Legal presumption of ownership
- Right to use the ® symbol
- Statutory damages in infringement suits
- Easier enforcement against squatters/infringers
- Blocks other people from registering the mark

### When to file

**Option A: File now (1(b) intent-to-use).** Coral isn't selling anything yet, but USPTO lets you file with a *bona fide* intent to use. This reserves the mark from the filing date (your "constructive use" date becomes your priority date). You have 6 months + optional 6-month extensions (up to ~3 years total) to show actual use and convert to a registration.

- Cost: ~$350/class (TEAS Standard; there's a cheaper TEAS Plus at $250 with stricter rules)
- Pro: Locks priority date. Good protection.
- Con: Money out before you have revenue. Administrative work.

**Option B: File later (1(a) actual use).** Wait until Coral is actually selling/publishing something under the Afferium name. File then based on demonstrated use.

- Cost: same ~$350/class
- Pro: No wasted money if Coral pivots
- Con: You lose priority to anyone who files 1(b) in the meantime. Risky because *Afferium* is coinable — someone else could stumble on it.

**Recommendation:** **File 1(b) within 3-6 months** of domain registration, *if* Coral is still committed. Gives enough time to validate the direction without losing priority.

### Classes to file under

Trademark classes are categories of goods/services. You pay per class. For Afferium as an arts & entertainment holding brand:

- **Class 9** — recorded media (CDs, digital downloads, software) _— for the album_
- **Class 16** — printed matter (books, magazines) _— for the book_
- **Class 41** — entertainment services, publishing services, artist management _— for the company's core services_

Three classes = ~$1,050 total USPTO fees for a solo 1(b) filing. Add a trademark attorney (optional but recommended for a first filing) → budget $1,500-2,500 total.

Additional classes to consider later:
- **Class 35** — advertising, business management _— if Afferium becomes a real artist-representation business_
- **Class 42** — scientific and technology services (software as a service, design) _— if Afferium ever productizes anything_

### DIY vs. attorney

DIY (self-file at uspto.gov):
- Possible. USPTO has decent docs. TEAS Plus has rigid rules that keep you on rails.
- Risk: wrong class, wrong ID of goods/services, or a poorly described mark can waste your filing fee or narrow your rights.

Attorney (flat fee, ~$500-1,500 per class for filing + prosecution):
- Worth it for a first filing, especially for a company name you'll use forever.
- Find someone via the USPTO attorney database or a flat-fee service (LegalZoom, Gerben, etc.).
- Recommendation: one hour of consultation with a trademark attorney (~$300) before deciding DIY vs. full engagement.

## Entity formation — when and where

**Don't form an LLC until at least one of these is true:**
1. Afferium is earning revenue (contracts, sales, licensing)
2. Afferium has liability exposure (employees, venue bookings, shipping physical product)
3. Coral needs to separate Afferium's finances from personal for tax/banking reasons
4. A collaborator or investor requires it

**When it's time:**
- **Delaware LLC** — flagship choice for companies that may raise outside capital later. $90 + $300/yr franchise tax. Needs a registered agent (~$100-300/yr).
- **Wyoming LLC** — cheaper ($100 + $60/yr), strong privacy, no state income tax. Good for a holding company with no employees.
- **New York LLC** — if Coral operates in NY. Has a publication requirement that can cost $1,000+ depending on county.
- **Home-state LLC** — if Afferium's activity is primarily in one state, forming there is simplest.

Given Coral's NY timezone → likely NY operations → look closely at whether a Wyoming or Delaware LLC still needs to register as "foreign LLC" in NY (it does, if operating there). Sometimes the simplest answer is a home-state LLC.
