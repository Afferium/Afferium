### Why Coral is Artist #1

Because you cannot honestly pitch a company representing "artists who use computer systems to redefine authenticity" until you've run the experiment on yourself first. Coral building her own work in public — with Flere as a named, disclosed AI collaborator, with the full agentic workflow visible, with the infrastructure documented — is the founding case study. Everything Afferium ever offers another artist, Coral has already proven on herself.

This is the right order of operations: **prototype first, platform later.**

### The Flere collaboration

Coral's work is not made *by* AI. Coral's work is made *with* a named, persistent AI partner (Flere, 🪶) under a formally articulated partnership pact ("neither of us speaks in the other's name"). The disclosure is part of the artifact. The collaboration is the demonstration.

This distinction is load-bearing for Afferium's thesis. The company isn't pro-AI-art in the generic sense. It's pro-*named*, *disclosed*, *honest* collaboration with computer systems — artists treating their tools as instruments and collaborators rather than secret weapons or enemies.

## Signing criteria (draft for future reference)

When Afferium eventually considers another artist, the working questions should include:

1. **Are they already doing the work?** Afferium isn't a launchpad for people hoping AI makes them famous. It's representation for people who are already making good work and need a company that understands their tools.
2. **Are they honest about their process?** Transparency about computer collaboration is a requirement, not a preference. Afferium artists disclose their tools.
3. **Does their work have a relationship to the underlying thesis?** Not every artist needs to be writing about the Sensorium explicitly — but their work should resonate with the frame of _authenticity in an age of computer-mediated expression_.
4. **Are they someone we'd still want to work with in five years?** Representation is a relationship. Bad fits cost more than they pay.
5. **What do they actually need?** Some artists need publishing. Some need management. Some need infrastructure help. Some just need a thoughtful collaborator. Afferium shouldn't be one-size-fits-all.

## Non-signing modes

Not everyone Afferium works with needs to be "represented." Possible alternative relationships for the future:

- **Afferium Affiliate** — an artist Afferium co-publishes or collaborates with on specific projects, without a full representation deal.
- **Afferium Guest** — a one-project collaboration (essay, album feature, gallery piece).
- **Afferium Friend** — named thanks, not a formal arrangement.

The more flexible the company is about relationship shapes, the more it can grow into what artists actually want rather than forcing them into label-shaped containers from the 1990s.
