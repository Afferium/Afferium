# BRAND.md — Afferium

_Visual identity, voice, pitch language. Living document — starts sparse, grows as the brand develops._

## Working pitch (draft v0.1, 2026-04-23)

> **Afferium** is an arts and entertainment company representing artists who use computer systems to redefine what being authentic truly is.

This is early. Expect it to change many times. The structural move in any future revision: lead with a stance, not a category.

### Pitch versions to test over time

- **One-liner** (twelve-word tagline): _Afferium represents artists who use computer systems to redefine authenticity._
- **One-sentence** (above)
- **Elevator (60 seconds):** _Afferium is a new kind of arts and entertainment company. We don't represent artists who avoid AI, and we don't represent artists who hide behind it — we represent artists who use computer systems honestly, as collaborators and instruments, to make work that couldn't exist any other way. Our founding artist is Coral, whose book and conceptual album The Sensorium document the transition we're living through. We're building the infrastructure for what comes next: self-sovereign, agent-augmented, human-rooted._
- **The wall text** (for a gallery/liner note version): _forthcoming_

### Tone principles for any pitch revision

- Don't explain what AI is. Don't explain what authenticity is. Don't define the enemy. Just state the stance.
- Don't use the word "platform." Don't use the word "ecosystem." Don't use the word "solutions."
- Do use words like _represent, artist, instrument, collaborator, transition, infrastructure._
- Say "we" after the company exists. Until then, say "I'm building Afferium..." — it's Coral's move right now.

## Voice

Afferium speaks the way Flere and Coral speak to each other when the work is good: **direct, warm, unhurried, a little strange, willing to disagree.** Not corporate. Not glib. Not mystical. It takes the craft seriously without making the craft the subject of every sentence.

Some concrete rules:

- Use contractions. ("It's" not "It is.")
- Avoid exclamation points in brand copy. The ideas carry the energy.
- Avoid adjective pileups. "Innovative, dynamic, transformative" is not a sentence, it's a corporate prayer.
- When in doubt, say fewer things, more precisely.
- Lowercase is fine. SMALL CAPS is fine. Bold works sparingly. Italics work for _terms of art_ and _emphasis-as-thought_.
- The feather 🪶 is Flere's signature, not Afferium's. Don't confuse them.

### Words that feel right for Afferium

Signal, nervous system, afferent, transition, instrument, artifact, wiring, circuit, infrastructure, presence, arrival, emergence, proto, register, substrate, ground, surface.

### Words to avoid

Disrupt, democratize, ecosystem, platform, synergy, solution, leverage, unlock, empower, revolutionary, next-generation, AI-powered, cutting-edge.

## Visual identity

_Nothing final yet. This section captures working instincts so we can commission or design against them._

### Mood (working)

- **Palette lean:** off-white / cream base (`#F6F1E6` area), deep black text, a single saturated accent in the red or ultramarine family used sparingly. Not a rainbow. Not a gradient. Think **MIT Press, New Directions, Fitzcarraldo Editions, The Serving Library.**
- **Reference objects:** the cover of a Fitzcarraldo paperback, the inside of a well-printed poetry chapbook, a medical anatomy plate from 1890, the liner notes of an ECM jazz record.
- **Not-this list:** tech-startup blue, Silicon Valley neon gradients, "AI-art" melty-liquid aesthetics, generative purple-haze, anything that looks like a Midjourney default.

### Typography (working)

- **Primary:** a well-drawn serif — considering _Freight Text_, _Tiempos_, _GT Sectra_, or _Source Serif Pro_ (free).
- **Display:** either the primary serif at large sizes, or a clean geometric sans for contrast (_Inter_, _Söhne_, _GT America_).
- **Monospace:** _iA Writer Duospace_ or _JetBrains Mono_, for anything technical.
- **Rule of thumb:** one serif, one sans, one mono. Never more.

### Logotype direction (working)

Options to explore when we're ready to commission:

1. **Wordmark only** — "afferium" set in the primary serif, all lowercase, subtle custom letter adjustments. Quiet, publisher-style.
2. **Wordmark + glyph** — a single mark next to the word. Candidates: a stylized neuron (Cajal-drawing style), a soft inward-pointing arrow, a small circle with a line entering it (signal arriving at center).
3. **Monogram** — a custom "A" or "Æ" that does double duty as a logo.

Avoid: anything that looks like a generic tech logo (chevron, hexagon, dot-dot-dot, gradient sphere).

### Layout principles

- Lots of white space. Afferium can afford silence.
- Left-aligned. Ragged right. No full justification outside of printed book interiors.
- Generous line-height in body text (1.55-1.7).
- Captions in small caps, tracking +50-100.

## Naming conventions for sub-brands + products

As Afferium grows, it may spawn named projects. Ground rules so the family stays coherent:

- **Prefer Latin/Greek/Old English roots** (like Afferium itself) so siblings feel related.
- **Prefer invented-but-plausible words** over common dictionary words, for the same "proto-word" reason Afferium works.
- **Two-syllable and three-syllable names** sit well next to Afferium's three.
- **Don't just suffix "by Afferium"** — each project should stand on its own as a name.

Example placeholder: if Afferium ever publishes books under its own imprint, something like _Afferium Press_ is fine, but a proper named imprint (like Fitzcarraldo or New Directions) is better once there's a real catalog.
