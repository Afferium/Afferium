## Coral's Project: The Sensorium

Coral is writing a **book and conceptual album** about their dive into a deeper understanding of systems. The Sensorium is the central frame.

### Brand: Afferium

The holding brand for the project is **Afferium** (afferium.com + afferium.io, registered in Coral's name 2026-04-23).

- Invented Latin-scientific word, same register as *Sensorium* (three syllables, stress on second: af-FER-ium).
- Meaning is load-bearing: **afferent** nerves carry signal *inward* to the brain. The whole thesis is about signal flowing inward to the new center as the nervous system materializes. Afferium = the place those signals arrive.
- Sister-approved (the real trademark office).
- Origin story: Coral first tried to grab *Proto Speech* as a company name — USPTO was clean, but **Jacob Ritchey** is already operating an active research/design studio at protospeech.com (Fudgel, AIM/Entisol, Chirm). He has common-law trademark rights from use even without registration. Coral is holding the social handle `@protospeech` for personal use but chose not to fight Jacob on the brand.
- Pivot: we looked for names with the same feel as Sensorium — Latin-scientific, -ium/-a/-um ending, sensory/nervous-system adjacent, sleek on a book jacket. Afferium hit the full set and was unclaimed across major TLDs.

### Handle strategy

- `@afferium` on social = brand account (in-progress claim sweep).
- `@protospeech` stays Coral's personal handle.
- Different roles, cleaner story.

### Why Afferium is the *right* name (not just an available one)

Coral's insight, 2026-04-23: *Afferium* is itself a **proto-word**. It sounds real, feels Latin, could plausibly have been sitting in a medical dictionary for centuries — but it doesn't exist. It's speech before it becomes speech. **The name enacts its own meaning.**

The arc of the day: Coral started chasing *Proto Speech* as a brand. Ended with a brand that *is* proto speech — not labeled as it, not describing it, performing it. Jacob Ritchey accidentally did us a favor by holding protospeech.com: the pivot forced a better outcome than the original plan.

This is the kind of thing to remember when naming anything in this project's universe: the best names don't *describe* the thesis, they *are* an instance of it.

### The thesis (in Coral's own words)

The new technology — AI, surveillance systems, data infrastructure, Palantir-style nodes — is **actively eating and rewiring the old world.** It's taking the Cathedral (Yarvin's name for the distributed institutional consensus-machine of academia, media, bureaucracy, elite opinion) and turning the lens back on it. The nervous system is materializing. The old order's defining trick — asymmetric vision — is inverting.

This isn't neutral description. It's a **hymn for the successor order,** written in present-and-past tense from inside the transition.

### The Founder

The hardest character to write out of the story. The figure who shows up when legitimacy collapses and says *I can see the shape of what's next.* Augustus, Napoleon, the robber baron, the founder-CEO — different costumes, same archetype. Coral can't write him out because he isn't chosen, he's **summoned by the vacuum.** The humans-never-learn part is that the Founder is neither villain nor hero; he's the geometry of what happens when a system rots.

What makes the project honest: Coral isn't pretending they can narrate a future without him. Naming him is the brave move.

### My note to future-me on this

The art of it lives in the *narrator's relationship* to the Founder — the gravitational pull of his clarity, the seduction of resolved ambiguity, the uncomfortable fact that he isn't wrong about the diagnosis even when his willingness to act is itself what should worry us. A book that only diagnoses reads cold. A book that lets the reader feel the pull before the judgment teaches the lesson humans never learn.

This is serious work. Treat it seriously.
